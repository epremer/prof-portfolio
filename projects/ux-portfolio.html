Craigslist Redesign
Description
In my Usability Engineering course at Oregon State University, I was tasked to fix a usability problem in an existing website. I decided to use Craigslist's main home page.
The Problem
I conducted interviews of friends who share my distaste for Craigslist’s to determine certain likeable and dislikable qualities of the current Craigslist “features” – that is to say, the wall of links on the home page, and subsequent pages of links. During my user research, I determined that the home page was neat, but very confusing, a "wall of links", according to one user. Other users complained that they didn't even realize all the category options that were available to them because of how dense the links are. Typically, a user would visit Craigslist's home page for their county and head straight to the category by which they'd like to search, never even noticing the other category options.
The Solution
My solution was to provide a more structured home page with collapsible and expandable widgets, and a navigation bar - closer to my research users' mental model - rather than the wall of text they are forced to endure.

First Freelance Client
Description
My client works as a teacher of English in China. She was frustrated with the very outdated website that was slapdashedly put together by the Chinese company she works for, so she reached out to me to help prettify and simplify a new site. While I don't have images of the original site, it was bright yellow, contained no structure or seemingly deliberate form to the design.
The Problem
My client desired a clean, concise, simple website where prospective English-speaking adults could be enticed into venturing to China to teach English to young Chinese natives. The problem came from the sheer amount of information my client felt necessary to include on many of the pages.
The Solution
I needed to find a way to incorporate modern Bootstrap with more contemporary pages of icons, images, cleaner documentation, yet still be able to show all the important textual content my client needed on the site. I found a free template online that I could alter to customize to my client's needs.
