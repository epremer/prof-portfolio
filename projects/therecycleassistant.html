The Recycle Assistant
10 June 2018
Description
The Recycle Assistant started as an individual project proposal for my CS361 Software Engineering I class through Oregon State University. I have always had a passion for proper recycling and waste management.
The Problem
Recently, I was at a local Costco and as I was desposing of the container to a sample, I noticed the trash/recycle bin combo (which had an opening for trash and a second opening for recycling) was really just a single container with two openings. The two holes led to the same trash bag! I "joked" with a nearby employee to which she responded, "No one was using the recycle part correctly, so we just combined them."

I run into this type of situation all the time. This defeatist way of thinking is enabling thousands of tons of recyclable materials to be dumped into landfills (and therefore not actually recycled) all over the world.

There seems to be a common miseducation - or perhaps lack of education where it pertains to proper recycling habits.
The Solution
Since so many people are unwilling to comply with proper recycling habits - or perhaps they are naive to proper techinques? - I have developed the beginnings of a device that will not only properly categorize recyclable materials from other categories of recyclables and waste, but simultaneously (re)educate consumers in those proper materials' final destinations. (Or rather, those materials' next step in order to continue their recycling journey.)
Preparation
This device is an Internet of Things device, connected via mesh network to its sister devices, so it will need to have those necessary capabilities.

It may also require the support of malls, theaters, and areas of large populous in order to gain momentum and popularity among the masses.
Any Snags?
Since I do not fully have the necessary abilities to fully develop this device, it may be a project I pursue more fully at a later time.
Final Product:
There is no final product as of yet, as this is simply a proposal. However, I would love to make this a real project that can legitimately be used to reeducate consumers about recycling and incentivize keeping recyclable materials out of our landfills. Click here to view full Vision Statement
