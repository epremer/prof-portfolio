My Cake Creations
10 June 2018
Description
I decided to make my first 3D cake for a former-boyfriend who loved camels. I had no idea what I was doing and thought it would be so simple. It's just bake a cake, shape it, and add frosting... right?

I found that to be terribly, terribly wrong. The first attempt at my camel cake was more like a large pile of orangy-brown cake with eyes. Don't get me wrong, it was delicious! But it resembled nothing like a real creature.

My godmom is adept at creating beautiful cakes with fondant, and we connected instantly over shows like Ace of Cakes which follow Charm City Cakes. I saw this show and knew I wanted to learn to do that.

After just one small, round cake practice, I attempted a second camel cake - my third any-kind-of-cake attempt. The results speak for themselves.
The Process
Without going into too much detail about how to build 3D cakes, I will say it requires patience and knowledge of structural integrity necessities. See images below for partial steps and final products.
Preparation
Especially for the camel and sloth cakes, I had to figure out what the structure holding the cake would need to be like. A strong base is the most necessary, especially for all the metal parts and rice treats used to hide the mechanics.
What does this have to do with CS?
Well, directly, aboslutely nothing.

But...! When you look closer, not only am I insanely proud of these creations, this form of expression fulfills my aptitude for building 3D structures. What I've realized is that even with programming, I can still build cool, even beautiful, things that utilize software in their construction.

My goal is to create a mechatronic cake! Combine the software I'm learning, the robotics I hope to learn when I move closer to campus and can join the Robotics Club, and my love for building cakes.
